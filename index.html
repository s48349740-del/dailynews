Floods in Uttarakhand

The 2025 Uttarakhand flash flood occurred on 5 August 2025 in Uttarkashi, Uttarakhand, India. The disaster killed at least five people and left more than 50 others missing. Cause: Cloudburst initially reported; glacial lake outburst flood, glacier collapse, landslide or combination possible. Casualties: Widespread destruction in Dharali village. 5 fatalities confirmed, 50+ missing. 70 presumed dead by 14 August. Rescue: Indian Army, NDRF, SDRF mobilized. 190 people rescued. Helicopters on standby. Relief operations ongoing. Aftermath: Connectivity disrupted to Gangotri Dham. Char Dham Yatra affected. Second cloudburst at Sukhi Top. Temporary lake formed upstream in Harsil.

Landslides in Uttarakhand

Active landslides in September 2025 on Yamunotri Highway, Uttarkashi-Gangnani Road, and Dehradun due to heavy rains/cloudbursts. Specific incidents: - Yamunotri Highway: Impassable sections. - Uttarkashi-Gangnani Road: Disrupted traffic. - Dehradun: Cloudburst caused landslides. - Nainital: Hills unstable. - Rishikesh Neelkanth road (Aug 2025): 2 injured, 2 missing. - Rishikesh-Badrinath highway (Aug 2025): 3 deaths, highway blocked, 1500 pilgrims stranded. Causes: - Fragile mountain terrain - Heavy monsoon rains - Climate change - Unregulated construction
